Before you ask

Common questions

Why are batches limited to 20 students?

Because Parab Sir teaches every batch himself and wants to know each student by name. Twenty is the most one teacher can truly look after — so we never cross it, even when there's a waiting list.

What is the daily doubt hour?

One hour each day kept aside only for questions. No new lessons, no slides — just clearing whatever didn't click in class, individually if needed.

Can fees be paid in instalments?

Yes. We understand families have different needs, and most fees can be split across the year. Please speak to us directly so we can work it out together. (Final terms confirmed at the office.)

Do you offer a trial class?

You're welcome to sit in on one class before reserving a seat, subject to availability in that batch. Reach out through the contact page to arrange it.

What if my child joins mid-year?

If a seat is open, Parab Sir personally plans a short catch-up so the student isn't left behind. Mid-year fees are adjusted fairly for the remaining term.
