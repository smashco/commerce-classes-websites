Before you ask

Questions parents usually have

Why do you cap batches at 20 students?

Because I teach every batch myself and I want to know each student by name and by weakness. Twenty is the most one teacher can truly look after — so I never cross it, even when there's a waiting list and a fee on the table.

Is it really you teaching, or assistants?

It is me, every single session. No junior tutors, no recorded videos. When you join, the teacher you met is the teacher you keep for the whole year.

What exactly is the daily doubt hour?

One hour, every class day, kept aside only for questions — usually 7–8 pm. No new lessons, no slides. Just clearing whatever didn't click, one-to-one if that's what it takes. And you have my personal number for the doubts that come up later in the evening.

Can the fees be paid in instalments?

Yes. Each course has a standard two- or three-part plan, and if a family needs it spread further, we work that out together — quietly and without fuss. (Final terms confirmed at the office.)

Can my child sit in on a class first?

You're welcome to attend one class before reserving a seat, subject to space in that batch. Reach out through the contact page and I'll arrange a day.

What if my child joins part-way through the year?

If a seat is free, I personally plan a short catch-up so your child isn't left behind, and the fee is adjusted fairly for the remaining term.
