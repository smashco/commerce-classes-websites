Dear parent and student,

I'd like to tell you, in my own words, how I run this class.

I started teaching in 2009, and I made one rule I have never broken: I never take more than 20 students in a batch. Twenty is the most one teacher can truly look after, and I would rather turn away a fee than stop knowing each child by name.

I teach every single lecture myself — there are no junior tutors, no recorded videos standing in for me. I mark every test by hand and write what I'd like you to fix in the margin. Once a month I telephone your parents personally, not to read out marks, but to tell them honestly how you are doing and what we'll work on next.

And every student who joins gets my personal mobile number. If a sum won't come right at nine in the evening, you call me. That is the whole idea of this place: a small room, a teacher who stays until it makes sense, and a door that is always open for one more doubt.

If that sounds like the kind of teaching you've been looking for, come and meet me. There are only a handful of seats each year, and I'd be glad to keep one for you.

A page from the class diary

What a week with me looks like

Same room, same teacher, six evenings a week. The 7–8 pm doubt hour is held every day and belongs entirely to your questions.

Monday New chapter, taught slowly from first principlesAccountancy — we build the concept before touching a single sum
Tuesday Worked problems together on the boardYou attempt, I correct on the spot, no one moves on confused
Wednesday Economics — theory with real Indian examplesBudgets, markets and prices you actually see around you
Thursday Practice set + last week's test returned, hand-markedI go over the common mistakes I saw, one by one
Friday Weekly test under exam conditionsReal paper, real timing — so the board exam feels familiar
Saturday Revision & one-to-one catch-up for anyone behindIf you missed something this week, this is where we fix it
7–8 pm The daily doubt hour — every day, including SaturdayNo new lessons. Just you, me, and whatever didn't click

If a festival or exam shifts the week, I tell every parent in advance. Nothing about your child's term is left to a notice board.

Written down, so you can hold me to them

My promises to you

  1. Never more than 20 in a batch. When a batch is full, it is full for the year — even if there's a waiting list.
  2. I teach every session personally. No junior tutors, no recorded lectures. The teacher you meet is the teacher you get, all year.
  3. Printed chapter notes in your hand. Every chapter comes with my own notes and worked examples — nothing to copy frantically off the board.
  4. A weekly test with my handwritten feedback. I mark each paper myself and write, in the margin, exactly what to do differently next time.
  5. A monthly call to your parents. An honest phone call about how your child is really doing — not a number, a conversation.
  6. My personal number for doubts. Stuck on a sum in the evening? Call me. That access is part of joining, not an extra.
