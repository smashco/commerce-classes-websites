The Method

How a term is run

No mystery, no “magic” shortcuts. Four fixed habits, repeated every week of the year.

  1. 1

    A diagnostic test in week one

    Before teaching begins, every new student writes a diagnostic test in Accountancy and Mathematics. We use it to place each student honestly and to flag the chapters — usually depreciation and rectification of errors — that need rebuilding.

  2. 2

    Chapter-wise printed notes

    Each chapter comes with our own printed notes — worked formats, solved illustrations and a graded problem set. No dictation, no scrambling to copy the board. The notes are revised every year against the latest board paper pattern.

  3. 3

    A weekly Saturday test with a posted rank sheet

    Every Saturday the batch sits a one-hour test on the week’s chapter. Papers are returned within three days and a rank sheet is posted on the notice board. Students see exactly where they stand against the batch — and where the marks were lost.

  4. 4

    A monthly report to parents

    Once a month parents receive a written report: attendance, test scores, the rank trend and a specific note on what to watch at home. For board-year students we add a phone call before the preliminary examinations.
